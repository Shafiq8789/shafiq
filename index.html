ABOUT
LAUNCHPAD

Launchpad is a summer pre-accelerator program designed to help bold, curious, and action-oriented undergraduate students at Khalifa University turn their tech-driven ideas into real, working ventures.

Over the summer, five selected teams will go from concept to prototype with the support of expert mentors, hands-on workshops, and the tools they need to bring their ideas to life—culminating in a final pitch day.

At Khalifa University, we believe the next big ideas don’t come from perfect conditions—they come from students who are brave enough to try. That’s why we built Launchpad: a space where student ideas are taken seriously, creativity is matched with action, and business-building feels relevant, not rigid.

This isn’t about research for the sake of research. It’s about building practical, tech-forward solutions that matter—to you, your peers, and your community.

WHO SHOULD APPLY?

The Launchpad is open to KU undergraduate students who:

Have a business idea they’d like to explore that aligns with one of
the Launchpad challenge themes;

Are part of a team with at least one Emirati student;

Are ready to build a team made up of coders, creators, and problem-
solvers to balance the workload and bring their idea to life

Teams should include 3–4 members. While the above criteria are required, we strongly
encourage teams that bring diverse perspectives across nationality, gender, and skill

sets.Whether you’re an engineer with a product concept, an artist with a vision, or a coder
with curiosity - this space is yours!

HELP UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS NAVIGATE AN UNCERTAIN JOB MARKET?

WHAT'S IN IT FOR YOU?

Every selected student or team will be able to:

Get matched with industry experts, startup founders, and product
designers for one-on-one mentorship.

Join high-impact training sessions that take you from idea to mid-high
fidelity clickable prototypes.

Receive funding to help you build and test your prototype.

Pitch their venture at our final Showcase Day and gain visibility through KU
platforms and networks.

Build a startup-ready skillset including design thinking, branding, validation,
and storytelling.

Connect and collaborate with peers from different backgrounds while
working on something practical and hands-on.

And most importantly — gain the confidence to turn “I have an idea” into “I made this happen.”
